The books I've read in 2024

2024-12-01
3 minute read
library
books

These aren’t all the books I’ve read in 2024, but they are the ones that have captured my attention in a meaningful way. From history and philosophy to myth and mystery, each of these works has offered something unique. Whether exploring the revolutionary ideas of Jefferson and Paine in The Apostles of Revolution, delving into the depths of the human psyche with Programmed to Kill, or unraveling the myths and legends in The Poetic Edda and The Prose Edda, these books have sparked my curiosity and expanded my understanding. Other reads like There Is a River: The Story of Edgar Cayce and The Rosicrucian Enlightenment have offered insight into spiritual movements, while Metamorphoses and The Swann Way have taken me on philosophical and literary journeys that continue to resonate. These are the books that have stood out in 2024.

The Apostles of Revolution: Jefferson, Paine, Monroe, and the Struggle Against the Old Order in America and Europe by John Ferling

John Ferling’s The Apostles of Revolution is a masterfully written exploration of how the lives and ideas of Thomas Jefferson, Thomas Paine, and James Monroe helped shape not only the American Revolution but also the broader struggle for liberty across Europe. Reading it gave me a deeper understanding of the revolutionary era—not just as a series of political events, but as a moment of profound ideological transformation with global consequences.

Programmed to Kill: The Politics of Serial Murder by David McGowan

Programmed to Kill was an unsettling yet eye-opening experience that forced me to reconsider everything I thought I knew about serial killers and the broader societal forces at play. David McGowan’s core argument—that many infamous serial murders might not be the work of lone, psychopathic individuals but instead tied to larger, organised conspiracies—left me grappling with some profoundly uncomfortable questions.

The Poetic Edda & The Prose Edda (Complete Edition), by Sæmundr Sigfússon, Snorri Sturluson

Reading The Poetic Edda and The Prose Edda was like opening a gateway to the mythological heart of Norse culture. These works, brimming with gods, giants, and epic tales of creation and destruction, taught me not only about the myths themselves but also about the worldview and values of the societies that created them.

There Is a River: The Story of Edgar Cayce by Joseph Sugrue

There Is a River: The Story of Edgar Cayce by Joseph Sugrue is a fascinating and comprehensive biography of one of the most intriguing figures in the fields of psychic phenomena and holistic healing: Edgar Cayce. Through this book, I gained a deeper understanding of Cayce’s life, his extraordinary abilities, and the profound impact his work had on both the spiritual and medical communities. What I learned from Sugrue’s account is not only about the events of Cayce’s life but also about the power of human potential and the mystery of the mind.

The Rosicrucian Enlightenment by Frances A. Yates

Frances A. Yates’ The Rosicrucian Enlightenment opened my eyes to a fascinating but often overlooked chapter of history where mysticism, science, and politics converged. The book is not just a historical study; it’s an exploration of how ideas, even esoteric ones, can shape the course of history.

Metamorphoses by Ovid

Ovid’s Metamorphoses is a sweeping tapestry of myths that explore the transformative power of love, ambition, revenge, and the divine. Spanning creation to Julius Caesar’s apotheosis, it’s not just a collection of myths but a reflection on the fluidity of existence itself. Reading it was like witnessing the world in perpetual motion, where nothing is fixed and change is the only constant.

The Swann Way by Marcel Proust

Reading Marcel Proust’s Swann’s Way, the first volume of In Search of Lost Time, was like stepping into a labyrinth of memory, desire, and the passage of time. Proust’s writing is dense, reflective, and unhurried, and it taught me to approach life and literature in a way that values depth over speed.
